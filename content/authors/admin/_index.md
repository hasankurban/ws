<div style="text-align: justify">

Hasan Kurban is an Assistant Professor at <a href="https://www.hbku.edu.qa/en">Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU)</a> and Founding Director of the <a href="https://kurbanintelligencelab.com">Kurban Intelligence Lab (KIL)</a>, with adjunct appointments at <a href="https://www.indiana.edu/">Indiana University Bloomington</a> and <a href="https://www.qatar.tamu.edu/">Texas A&amp;M University at Qatar</a>. His research investigates the foundations of modern artificial intelligence, with particular emphasis on the reliability, interpretability, and scientific applicability of large-scale models.

<h2 style="margin-top: 2.2em; margin-bottom: 0.7em; font-weight: 600; letter-spacing: 0.01em; padding-bottom: 0.3em; border-bottom: 1px solid #d8d8d8;">Research</h2>

<p><strong>Trustworthy AI: Safety, Alignment, and Interpretability.</strong> Trustworthy foundation models with rigorous guarantees on reliability and behavior. Topics include hallucination detection and mitigation, sparse-autoencoder-based feature analysis, model editing, uncertainty quantification, calibration, jailbreak robustness, and red-teaming, with methodological emphasis on auditable behavior in multimodal and scientific systems.</p>

<p><strong>Reasoning and Inference-Time Compute.</strong> Mathematical, logical, and procedural reasoning in language and multimodal models under the test-time scaling paradigm. Topics include reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR), abstention-aware reasoning, posterior-constrained inference, multi-agent reasoning frameworks, neurosymbolic methods, and the robustness of chain-of-thought to noisy, conflicting, or adversarial evidence.</p>

<p><strong>Agentic AI and LLM Agents.</strong> Long-horizon autonomous agents with tool use, planning, and verifiable behavior. Topics include scientific discovery agents for quantum chemistry and materials, biomedical and healthcare agents, retrieval-augmented and knowledge-grounded reasoning, LLM-as-a-judge evaluation, agent safety, and multi-agent coordination across heterogeneous knowledge sources.</p>

<p><strong>Multimodal Foundation Models.</strong> Cross-modal grounding and reasoning over language, vision, and structured scientific data. Topics include vision&ndash;language models, video understanding and long-context temporal reasoning, world models for physical systems, embodied and spatial reasoning, neural rendering and Gaussian splatting, and multimodal benchmarks for crystalline materials, medical imaging, and quantum chemistry.</p>

<p><strong>AI for Science.</strong> Foundation models and agentic systems for autonomous scientific discovery. Application areas include materials informatics and quantum chemistry (with equivariant interatomic potentials and foundation models for crystalline systems), biomedical imaging, hydrogen storage and energy systems, and physics-informed and symmetry-aware learning.</p>
