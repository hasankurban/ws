Hasan Kurban is an Assistant Professor at <a href="https://www.hbku.edu.qa/en">Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU)</a> and Founding Director of the <a href="https://kurbanintelligencelab.com">Kurban Intelligence Lab (KIL)</a>, with adjunct appointments at <a href="https://www.indiana.edu/">Indiana University Bloomington</a> and <a href="https://www.qatar.tamu.edu/">Texas A&amp;M University at Qatar</a>. His research investigates the foundations of <strong>trustworthy and interpretable large-scale AI systems</strong>, with secondary interests in reasoning and inference-time compute, agentic AI, multimodal foundation models, and AI for science.

<h2 style="margin-top: 2.4em; margin-bottom: 0.8em; font-weight: 600; letter-spacing: 0.01em; padding-bottom: 0.35em; border-bottom: 1px solid #d8d8d8;">Primary Research Direction</h2>

<p><strong>Trustworthy AI and Foundation Model Interpretability.</strong> Building large-scale AI systems whose behavior can be measured, audited, and trusted. Current research investigates hallucination detection and mitigation in large language models, sparse-autoencoder-based feature analysis, model editing, uncertainty quantification and calibration, and red-teaming protocols for foundation models. Methodological emphasis is placed on auditable behavior in multimodal and scientific systems, in alignment with the priorities of the U.S. National Science Foundation and emerging AI safety institutes.</p>

<h2 style="margin-top: 2.4em; margin-bottom: 0.8em; font-weight: 600; letter-spacing: 0.01em; padding-bottom: 0.35em; border-bottom: 1px solid #d8d8d8;">Secondary Research Interests</h2>

<p><strong>Reasoning and Inference-Time Compute.</strong> Mathematical, logical, and procedural reasoning in language and multimodal models under the test-time scaling paradigm, including reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR), abstention-aware reasoning, posterior-constrained inference, multi-agent reasoning frameworks, and the robustness of chain-of-thought to noisy, conflicting, or adversarial evidence.</p>

<p><strong>Agentic AI and LLM Agents.</strong> Long-horizon autonomous agents with tool use, planning, retrieval-augmented and knowledge-grounded reasoning, LLM-as-a-judge evaluation, agent safety, and multi-agent coordination across heterogeneous knowledge sources, with applications to scientific discovery in quantum chemistry, materials, and biomedical domains.</p>

<p><strong>Multimodal Foundation Models.</strong> Cross-modal grounding and reasoning over language, vision, and structured scientific data, including vision&ndash;language models, video understanding and long-context temporal reasoning, world models for physical systems, and embodied and spatial reasoning, with multimodal benchmarks for crystalline materials, medical imaging, and quantum chemistry.</p>

<p><strong>AI for Science.</strong> Foundation models and agentic systems for autonomous scientific discovery, including scientific foundation models, equivariant interatomic potentials, physics-informed and symmetry-aware learning, and active learning for materials informatics, quantum chemistry, biomedical imaging, and energy systems.</p>
