<p style="margin-bottom: 1.3em;">Hasan Kurban is an Assistant Professor at <a href="https://www.hbku.edu.qa/en" style="text-decoration: none; border-bottom: 1px solid rgba(26,49,82,0.4);">Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU)</a> and Founding Director of the <a href="https://kurbanintelligencelab.com" style="text-decoration: none; border-bottom: 1px solid rgba(26,49,82,0.4);">Kurban Intelligence Lab (KIL)</a>, with adjunct appointments at <a href="https://www.indiana.edu/" style="text-decoration: none; border-bottom: 1px solid rgba(26,49,82,0.4);">Indiana University Bloomington</a> and <a href="https://www.qatar.tamu.edu/" style="text-decoration: none; border-bottom: 1px solid rgba(26,49,82,0.4);">Texas A&amp;M University at Qatar</a>. His research investigates the foundations of trustworthy and interpretable large-scale AI systems, drawing on methods from machine learning, statistical inference, and the natural sciences. Ongoing work in the lab spans trustworthy AI and foundation model interpretability, reasoning and inference-time compute, agentic AI and LLM agents, multimodal foundation models, and AI for science &mdash; with applications to materials informatics, quantum chemistry, biomedical imaging, and energy systems. The lab's work has appeared at <em>NeurIPS</em>, <em>ICML</em>, <em>ICLR</em>, <em>CVPR</em>, <em>ACL</em>, <em>EMNLP</em>, and <em>KDD</em>, and in <em>IEEE Transactions</em>, <em>Machine Learning</em>, and <em>Nature</em>-family journals.</p>

<p style="margin-bottom: 1.3em;">He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science with a minor in Statistics from Indiana University Bloomington in 2017, serves on the editorial board of a <em>Nature</em>-family journal, and reviews regularly for leading AI and data-science venues. Research in the lab is supported by the Qatar Research Development and Innovation Council, TÜBİTAK, and partner research universities.</p>
