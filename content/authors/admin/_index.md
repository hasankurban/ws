Hasan Kurban is an Assistant Professor at <a href="https://www.hbku.edu.qa/en">Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU)</a> and Founding Director of the <a href="https://kurbanintelligencelab.com">Kurban Intelligence Lab (KIL)</a>, with adjunct appointments at <a href="https://www.indiana.edu/">Indiana University Bloomington</a> and <a href="https://www.qatar.tamu.edu/">Texas A&amp;M University at Qatar</a>. His research investigates the foundations of trustworthy and interpretable large-scale AI systems, drawing on methods from machine learning, statistical inference, and the natural sciences.

<h2 style="margin-top: 2.4em; margin-bottom: 0.8em; font-weight: 600; letter-spacing: 0.01em; padding-bottom: 0.35em; border-bottom: 1px solid #d8d8d8;">Research</h2>

<p><strong>Trustworthy AI and Foundation Model Interpretability.</strong> The behavior of large language and multimodal models remains incompletely characterized, posing fundamental challenges for their reliable deployment. Research in the lab addresses hallucination detection and mitigation, sparse-autoencoder-based feature analysis, model editing, uncertainty quantification and calibration, and red-teaming, with methodological emphasis on auditable behavior in multimodal and scientific systems.</p>

<p><strong>Reasoning and Inference-Time Compute.</strong> Modern foundation models exhibit increasingly sophisticated reasoning under the test-time scaling paradigm, yet the formal properties of these procedures are not well understood. Topics of interest include reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR), abstention-aware reasoning, posterior-constrained inference, multi-agent reasoning frameworks, and the robustness of chain-of-thought to noisy, conflicting, or adversarial evidence.</p>

<p><strong>Agentic AI and LLM Agents.</strong> Autonomous agents built on foundation models extend their capabilities to long-horizon decision-making and external action. Research interests include tool use and planning, retrieval-augmented and knowledge-grounded reasoning, LLM-as-a-judge evaluation, agent safety, and multi-agent coordination across heterogeneous knowledge sources, with applications to scientific discovery in quantum chemistry, materials, and biomedical domains.</p>

<p><strong>Multimodal Foundation Models.</strong> Cross-modal grounding remains a central problem in modern AI, particularly for scientific data that combines language, vision, and structured representations. Research interests include vision&ndash;language models, video understanding and long-context temporal reasoning, world models for physical systems, embodied and spatial reasoning, and multimodal benchmarks for crystalline materials, medical imaging, and quantum chemistry.</p>

<p><strong>AI for Science.</strong> Foundation models and agentic systems are reshaping how scientific discovery is conducted across disciplines. Research interests include scientific foundation models, equivariant interatomic potentials, physics-informed and symmetry-aware learning, and active learning, with applications to materials informatics, quantum chemistry, biomedical imaging, and energy systems.</p>
